# The Signature Post Template

Every post, same six beats, same order. Readers should be able to predict the shape — the shape IS the brand. 400–1000 words.

## The six beats

1. **Hook — the gap.** One or two sentences. The distance between owning (or being curious about) the thing and actually understanding it: "I own shares in [company]. Yesterday I learned I couldn't answer: who actually pays them?" or "I cross this junction every day and had no idea who decides when the lights change." Never a generic topic intro ("Optical networking is a fascinating industry…" is banned).

2. **The question.** Stated in one plain sentence. This is the burst's framed question, verbatim or near it.

3. **The answer at my current level.** What the user now understands, in their own words, with exactly **one visual** — one, not zero, not two. It's chosen during draft/critique from the learning visuals in the workspace's Visuals tab (the rest stay there for understanding) and restyled clean-not-glossy. Hand-drawn schema, simple labelled chart, or tidy table all fit; polished consulting charts signal fake expertise.

4. **What surprised me / what I got wrong.** Mandatory, even if small. This is the honesty beat and the differentiator from every analyst post on X. If a takeaway genuinely earns a place, it goes here as one sentence on top ("If you remember one thing: X. What I got wrong though: Y") — subordinate to the surprise, never replacing it.

5. **Open loop.** Name the next question on the ladder. Every post ends pointing at the next one — this is the signature move that builds the series mechanically and gives readers a reason to follow rather than just like.

6. **The Kitchen.** Fixed footer, fixed name. The ONE prompt that cracked this post's question + one line on what it did and which model/tool ran it. Framing: "this is what I asked" — never "run this to replicate my results" (model, date, and follow-ups make replication claims dishonest). Full log lives in `prompts.md`, linked for the rare reader who wants it.

## Banned moves

- Jargon without a one-line plain-language translation immediately after first use
- More than one visual
- Claims without a source (or without an explicit "my guess:" label)
- Expert posture: recommendations, price targets, "investors should…" — the voice is "here's what I now understand", not "here's what you should do"
- Prompt dumps — one prompt max in the Kitchen
- Closed endings — a post that doesn't name the next question is unfinished

## HEIT mapping (completeness check, not a replacement)

Hook/Explain/Illustrate map to beats 1 and 3 — no post ships without a clear Explain and an Illustrate. But beats 4–5 (surprise + open loop) must never be swapped for a generic "Takeaway"; they are the signature. Pure HEIT produces interchangeable explainer content — exactly what this series is not.

## Voice rules

The voice is conversational — a person thinking out loud, not an analyst presenting. Unfinished thoughts, abrupt turns, and informal phrasing are features. Grammar and polish are never the bar; never critique toward them.

Argue, don't explain. A post advances ONE claim the author believes; explanation appears only in service of it. "Here's how X works" is not a post — "here's what I think X means / why X is mispriced / why everyone's wrong about X" is. The author's chronic tendency is to explain the world and close every open end; resist it. Run the subtraction test: delete every sentence that only explains — what's left is the post. If nothing's left, there's no post yet. Leave open ends on purpose; on social they're bait for replies, and a tidy conclusion just ends the conversation.

Originality over summary: every post needs at least one opinion that is genuinely the author's, labeled as theirs ("my take:"). Sentences that ventriloquize sources (10-K language, analyst phrasing, Wikipedia cadence) get flagged and challenged: "is this what you think, or what you read?" A beginner's honest opinion beats a fluent summary of other people's views — that's the product. Completeness is the enemy: every caveat added to feel thorough dilutes the claim.

Style exemplars in `deep-dives/style/` set a direction (rhythm, directness, how to open and close), never a costume. Any suggestion that moves the draft closer to an exemplar but further from the author's own voice gets dropped.

## Editing stance

When editing a draft against this template: numbered comments tied to specific lines. Identify which beat is missing or weak and say why it matters. Never return a rewritten draft. The post must stay in the user's voice, imperfections included — completeness and honesty are the bar, not polish.
